Mission Statement

Computer Science for Kids (CS4K) aims to introduce elementary and middle school students to programming concepts. We work with students at a variety of skill levels to give them the knowledge and tools necessary to make their own programs. CS4K empowers volunteers to become effective educators and develop their own teaching style in response to the needs of students.

What We Do

Our goal is to give quality computer science education to students of all backgrounds. Our volunteers use programs such as: Scratch MIT, Swift Playgrounds, Dash & Dot robots, and more.

Depending on the program, students not only learn computer science fundamentals, but they also create projects of their own (following a theme) in the form of a game, animation, or anything else!

History

The Computer Science for Kids (CS4K) organization was founded in February of 2016 by Mariah Arevalo and Karen Chan. They created CS4K as an effort to bring computer science to underrepresented schools and get students excited about programming.

In April 2017, the board expanded from two co-presidents to a team of seven to support our mission statement.
